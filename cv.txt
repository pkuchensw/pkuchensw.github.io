    \section{Research Experience}       
        \vspace{0.1 cm}
        \begin{twocolentry}{            
        \textit{May 2025 - Present}}
            \textbf{Efficient Reinforcement Learning for Large Language Models}
            
            \textit{Advisor: Prof. Bin Cui and Prof. Xupeng Miao, Peking University}
        \end{twocolentry}
        \vspace{0.10 cm}
        \begin{onecolentry}
            \begin{highlights}
                \item Investigated efficiency bottlenecks in LLM reinforcement learning, including the straggler effect caused by long-tail rollout lengths and the length explosion problem in LLM RL training.
                
                \item Proposed DARTS (Distribution-Aware Active Rollout Trajectory Shaping), a system-level optimization paradigm that actively reshapes the model's output distribution to reduce intra-prompt redundancy. Designed Dual-End Sampling, Adaptive Redundant Allocation, and a token-level streaming mechanism to accelerate RL training while preserving reasoning capability.
                
                \item Proposed QLPO, a resampling-based variant of GRPO that introduces implicit length control by favoring short-correct and long-incorrect trajectories without modifying the reward function.
                
                %\item Demonstrated consistent improvements in RL training efficiency and the accuracy--length trade-off across models from 1.5B to 32B.
            \end{highlights}
        \end{onecolentry}
        
        \vspace{0.1 cm}
        \begin{twocolentry}{            
        \textit{Feb 2026 - Present}}
            \textbf{Efficient Diffusion Language Models}
            
            \textit{Advisor: Prof. Fan Lai, University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign}
        \end{twocolentry}
        
        \vspace{0.20 cm}
        \begin{onecolentry}
            \begin{highlights}
                \item Exploring diffusion language models for efficient parallel generation, focusing on improving the quality--efficiency trade-off beyond standard autoregressive and block-diffusion decoding.
                
                \item Investigating scalable training and inference designs that enhance self-consistency, long-block generation, and practical serving efficiency.
            \end{highlights}
        \end{onecolentry}
        
        \vspace{0.2 cm}
        \begin{twocolentry}{            
        \textit{Feb 2026 - May 2026}}
            \textbf{Context Management Benchmark for Long-Horizon LLM Agents}
            
            \textit{Advisor: Prof. Bin Cui and Prof. Xupeng Miao, Peking University}
        \end{twocolentry}
        
        \vspace{0.10 cm}
        \begin{onecolentry}
            \begin{highlights}
                \item Worked on MemClaw-Bench, a capability-centered benchmark for evaluating context management in long-horizon tool-using agents under compressed-context settings.
                
                \item Designed diagnostic tasks and evaluation protocols covering memory capabilities such as constraint tracking, version updating, procedure transfer, source conflict resolution, task resumption, and staleness judgment.
            \end{highlights}
        \end{onecolentry}
